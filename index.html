The Stars That Bled

The night Elowen found the dying star, it had already begun to bleed silver across the frost-dark sky. She pressed her palm flat against the tower's cold stone and watched the light fall — not like rain, not like snow, but like a secret the universe had kept too long and could no longer hold.

"You shouldn't be touching what isn't yours," said the voice from behind her. Low. Patient. The kind of voice that had never needed to raise itself because everything it wanted had always come to it willingly.

She didn't turn around. "And yet here I am."

A pause. The silver light painted the edges of her dark hair, and she felt — rather than heard — him step closer. The warmth of him was wrong against the winter air. Too deliberate. Too aware.

"Yes," he said at last. "Here you are."
